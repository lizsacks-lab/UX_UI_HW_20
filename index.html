Mestow Mobile App Case Study

Finding Your Next Favorite Show

Why Mestow?

Today, more people spend time watching over the top (OTT) streaming services like Netflix, Hulu and Amazon Prime than they do driving a car or talking to friends and family.

As of 2019, 65% of 18-34 year-olds were OTT subscribers.

On average, users access content on three different devices across three different OTT services.

(Source: Zype)

The Problem

As the volume of content offered across all of the separate OTT streaming services continues to grow, our schedules continue to fill and our heightened expectations for truly great content become "off the charts”, the need for TV viewers to find their next favorite show accurately and efficiently is more desired than ever.

How might we streamline the process for TV viewers, so they can access relevant recommendations tailored to their viewing unique habits for all OTT streaming services they subscribe to all in one place?

The Research

  • 1:1 Interviews

  • Survey

  • Competitive Research

Five participants within our target demographic of 18-34 years old who subscribed to multiple streaming platforms were interviewed. Questions focused on the search methods the participants used to find new TV shows and how well those methods ultimately fared for finding programs that fit their preferences.

Key Takeaways
“There are so many choices and new ones coming out every day.”

With limited time and so much content (old and new) people struggle to find that next best choice. And people genuinely want to find the best option for them, not settling for something that doesn’t fully satisfy their desires.

“I rarely go into it completely blind.”

Interviewees discussed using multiple resources to search for their next new show. Top methods included: Google searches for review sites, friend/family recommendations, online reviews and social media.

“I think [online reviewers] are all just so different.”

Interviewees were pretty divided on whether they read online reviews. Many expressed not finding reviews from people they don’t know personally to be valid. Others said they read them if there are a high enough volume and they could see some consistency in perspective.

“I get this empty feeling when I watch a series finale.”

People get emotionally invested and attached to shows that truly resonate with them.

In total 28 participants were surveyed, spanning a range of ages, ethnicities and educational levels to provide a broadened perspective among OTT subscribers’ viewing habits. Questions focused on what resources they use to find new shows, then their viewing habits overall.

Key Takeaways

There are a few apps in the marketplace that streamline OTT streaming service viewing. Some specialized in the social component of following friends to chat and receive recommendations on shows, while others built recommendations based upon what a viewer has previously watched. Our team wanted to build an app that could combine all of these features and satisfy all user needs.

The Concept

Mestow accurately recommends shows to viewers across all the OTT streaming services in which they subscribe by having them complete a new user rating of 20 shows upon sign-up. Users are encouraged to rate a range of shows they like/dislike through a 5-star rating process so the app can more accurately recommend shows. Within the app, users can save shows to a watchlist, follow friends and family to view their watchlists, and read online reviews that are exclusively written by Mestow’s loyal users. The app is designed for the discerning TV viewer who wants to filter through to the shows of their specific taste, in the most efficient manner possible.

Definition

User Persona
Adam Jenkins
Age: 37
Status: Married with 2 kids
Profession: Art Teacher

Goals:

Creating an ideal balance between work, time for his art and music, quality time with the kids, then whatever time is leftover, vegetation time.

Staying current with truly great entertainment whether it be art, live music or even video games...anything to satisfy his creative outlet.

Limitations:

Being passionate and having a range of interests makes Adam the amazing person he is but he’s always conflicted with a mix of excitement and hesitation when taking on a new challenge for fear of stretching himself too thin.

Although he loves spending quality time with this wife and kids, some of his favorite viewing preferences are ones his wife doesn’t care for and aren’t appropriate for the kids. He would welcome some more “me-time” to enjoy his own interests.

User Insight:

Adam loves how much quality programming exists today compared to even 5 years ago. He just wishes there was a more efficient way to find shows that match his specific preferences.

User Flow

Mestow’s user flow is designed to gather necessary data for users at initial sign-up to provide accurate show recommendations throughout the relationship of the app. The process from sign-up to home screen is 5-10 minutes at most, and should be enjoyable rather than a chore. Then, the user has the freedom to choose show recommendations however they prefer, whether it be Mestows’ picks, online reviews or viewing followers’ watchlists.

The Design Process

Wireframes

Three key screens within the Mestow app and their user experience improvements from low to high fidelity prototypes.

Genre Selection

Radio buttons were easy to mistakenly select and too small. Toggle switches were added to the high fidelity.

Home Screen

The TV show thumbnails were enlarged to be more readable and engaging, pus review and following icons were improved to be more straightforward.

TV Show Selection

The embedded video was enhanced with an auto-play feature for the show’s trailer. Then, rather than dropdown menus for each category, horizontal tabs were made for smoother navigation.
